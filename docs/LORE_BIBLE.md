# LORE BIBLE — The Sorcerer-Sword ARPG: The Ankuspawn Conspiracy
### Draft for Hiro's red pen. Anything marked ⚑ is my proposal, not established canon — approve, edit, or strike.

## 1. Era & Timeline (locked at checkpoint)
The game is set in the **Kingdom of Ankunyx, 25 years post–Book 4** — the same present as Cookie's, Holly's, and Shen Sama's campaigns. Kenji is **AnkuNyx, the Dragon Emperor**, absent for long stretches; stewards govern. Kingdom laws in force everywhere the player goes: vampires are citizens, orcs are licensed mercenaries, dwarves trade openly, **necromancy is illegal** except under license.

## 2. The Conspiracy (main quest spine)
The **Cult of Anku**, founded by **Lady Nyx** (the Lich Queen, wife of AnkuNyx). Two phases, both active and SECRET:
- **Phase 1:** cultists disguised as Bane-of-Eve encounters seduce Kenji and deliver his seed to Nyx at the **Ashenveil Academy**. He doesn't know they serve his wife.
- **Phase 2 (current, the game's subject):** the cult locates, profiles, and captures **mature Ankuspawn (18+)**, imprisoning them in the Academy's lower levels, harvesting blood/essence/Ember fragments by **Ember theme** (Verdance → fertility/resurrection elixirs, Veil → invisibility/memory-wipe, Resonance → charm, Nexus → domination…). Targeting is strategic: scouts profile by observed abilities; females are priority.

**Canon guardrail (hard rule):** the conspiracy is still secret in the live campaigns, so the game's ending may *wound it locally* — a cell destroyed, captives freed — but Nyx survives, the Academy operation survives, the secret holds, and **AnkuNyx never learns of it**. No named canon character dies.

## 3. Proposed main quest — 5 beats ⚑
1. **THE EMPTY CELL (arena/prologue):** the Pit's previous champion — quietly gifted — vanished the night after his last victory. Bellow grumbles about it between fights. The crowd thinks he ran. ⚑ The cult had him profiled; the player, racking up kills and a crowd-name, is now being watched too.
2. **THE LISTENING ROOM (Karridge Inn):** the innkeeper's rumor web — three more disappearances along the trade road, all "talented." A guest who asks too-specific questions about the player's abilities turns out to be a **cult scout**; confronting or tailing them yields a cipher pointing to the forest road.
3. **ROOTS THAT ROT (Thorn Grove):** a cult cell is using a **ley-line node** in the grove to move sedated captives. The wood elves know something is wrong with the forest's flow but not what. Break the cell's camp, free a captive — ⚑ a near-miss cameo: **Shen Sama**, hunted True Dragon Ankuspawn, passes through hiding from his mother; he refuses to stay, and warns the player what the cult really wants.
4. **THE BUYER (both zones):** evidence trail — elixir vials, a desperate buyer (⚑ Vess-pattern: a woman seeking a fertility elixir, not evil, just desperate) — reveals where the captives go: **the Ashenveil**, the Academy's lower levels. The player can't reach it (locked region) but can burn the local pipeline.
5. **ASH AND SILENCE (finale, Thorn Grove dungeon):** destroy the Karridge–Grove pipeline and free the captives in transit. The cult erases everything behind it. Closing scene ⚑: **AnkuNyx** appears in Karridge — disputes settle, the crowd kneels — and the player, holding proof that implicates the Emperor's own wife, must watch him leave without being able to prove a word. The conspiracy holds. (Sequel hook = your live campaigns.)

## 4. Geography
- **Karridge City** — NEW canon, original to this game. ⚑ Placement: a free trade-road city south of Varenholm near the old Sunderplains border; its fighting-pit culture grew from licensed orc mercenary companies mustering there. Torchlit, dark-ages, crowded.
- **The Pit of Karridge** — the arena; announcer **Bellow**; crowd-nickname tradition; payout per kill. (Canon per the HTML game.)
- **Thorn Grove** — ⚑ the friendly southern fringe of **Deepwood/Silvandris** (elven, the Eldest, internal autonomy). Giant roots, glowing flora, wood-elf settlement, a ley-line node.
- **Locked teasers at zone edges** ⚑ mapped to real lore regions: **Deepwood Interior** (the Eldest's domain), **Dragonspine Mountains** (dragon treaty; Vorathiel's flight), **Kharn-Dural** (dwarven undermountain), **The Ashenveil** (the Academy; the conspiracy's heart — greyed out with a deliberate "the way is barred").
- **Portals exist** (13, coalition territories) but the player has no access — true to lore that most people don't.

## 6. World systems the game uses
**Ley-line magic** — power conduits; the Grove node is quest-critical. **Guild contracting** — the Adventurers Guild board in Karridge is a licensed kingdom guild (kill-quests, small gold + potion). **Academy politics** — Varenholm vs Ashenveil rivalry; Ashenveil's dark reputation makes the conspiracy plausible deniability. **Ember magic** — Ankuspawn-unique; the *reason* for the harvest; the player character is NOT Ankuspawn ⚑ (keeps the three ported kits canon-clean).

## 9. THE SERAPHIM (added 2026-06-12, Hiro-directed 4th champion)
**Who he says he is:** a celestial of the place above — giant angel wings, three hound heads (cerberus-fashion), muscular human body, angelic cloak bound in runic chains at waist/chest/arms, a halo he can unleash as a linear ray. Great evil brews in the land (true — the Ankuspawn harvest IS brewing); he has come to find one worthy warrior to join the campaign above.
**Who he actually serves (never shown to the player):** he is a celestial entity in joint service to the **god of death** and the **god of creation** — "the one who closes doors, and the one who opens them" (shrine text alludes, never names). The gods of nature and entropy from the books' tracker files / Book 1 remain alluded context. Whether his recruiting drive is holy duty or something considerably more self-interested (Hiro's "space babies and a personal harem" theory) is deliberately left unconfirmed — King Nibnob voices the suspicion in-game and is dismissed by nobody.
**His road (replaces the conspiracy beats):** Pit → Marlow (awed, refuses payment) → spine trail east of Thorn Grove → treaty stone (bars all non-celestials; preserves the "treaty lands" lock for other champions) → **DRAGONSPINE** → duel the five banners → recruit ONE → Skyreach shrine ascension ending + credits. The conspiracy survives untouched; he simply never engages it (an angel does not chase rumors).
**The five banners:** Kargoth (ogre warchief), Skarva Red-Blade (orc blade-saint), King Nibnob the Thrice-Crowned (goblin king, duels with guards — "crown rules"), Vesshk of the High Wind (wyvern matriarch, speaks like weather via narrator), Aurvaeth the Treaty-Bound (dragon in traveler shape — Dragonspine's treaty dragons made playable-adjacent at last; Vorathiel remains distant canon, untouched).
**Canon guardrails:** no named book characters harmed or moved; Dragonspine treaty intact for the valley (Aurvaeth's loophole: "the sky above it was never named"); the NPC awe (chatter, Marlow, guild clerk) is local color, not doctrine.

## 10. THE WARLOCK'S EPILOGUE (added 2026-06-12, Hiro-directed)
The plaza finale was the audition. After AnkuNyx passes: the **White Order** (paladins, inquisitors, demon hunters — sanctioned) serves a kill-writ on the warlock in the plaza; he answers it the only way he answers anything. A sealless letter, the **Pale Courier** in the west-wall alley, the black carriage with dead horses — and **LADY NYX, revealed in person** at the Ashenveil academy, to the warlock ALONE (canon: she remains "the Matron"/unseen for everyone else; this reveal is his road's price and prize). The offer: hunt and CAPTURE Ankuspawn for the cult, in exchange for diplomatic protection — kill-writs die in committee when the crown's shadow countersigns. Accept-only (he NEEDS the protection after killing sanctioned men); two flavors of yes. New zone: **THE ASHENVEIL** (warlock-only): ash fields, the working dead, the academy ("the lower levels are NOT a metaphor"). Credits: "the writs die in committee, and the web has a new spider." Conspiracy intact; he now serves it knowingly — the campaigns inherit a cult with a warlock on retainer.
